How to Make Money on Etsy in the UK: A Beginner's Guide for 2026

Etsy is one of the best platforms for selling handmade, vintage, or digital products in the UK — here's how to set up, what to sell, and how to actually make sales.

What sells on Etsy in the UK?

Etsy is best known for handmade goods, vintage items, and digital downloads. The UK market is particularly strong for personalised gifts, home décor, jewellery, wedding stationery, and art prints. Digital products (planners, templates, printables, SVG files) are increasingly popular because there are no materials, postage, or handling costs — you make the product once and sell it indefinitely.

Before opening a shop, search Etsy for the type of products you plan to sell. Look at the bestsellers and reviews — what are they doing that you can do equally well or better? What gaps exist in the market?

Setting up your shop

Creating an Etsy shop takes about 30 minutes. Choose a shop name that reflects your niche — something memorable and searchable. Complete every section of your profile including your shop story, policies, and an attractive banner. A complete, professional-looking shop converts browsers into buyers.

Start with at least 10 listings — shops with more listings rank better in Etsy search and give visitors more reason to stay and browse. Quality photos are non-negotiable: natural light, consistent styling, and multiple angles for physical products.

Etsy SEO — how to get found

Etsy search works similarly to Google. Your listing title, tags, and description tell Etsy what your product is and who to show it to. Use all 13 tags available. Research what buyers actually search for — type your product type into the Etsy search bar and note the autocomplete suggestions. Those are real search terms people use.

Include your most important keywords in the first 40 characters of your title (that's what shows in search results). Renewing listings occasionally gives them a temporary search boost.

Understanding Etsy fees

Etsy charges a 20p listing fee per item, a 6.5% transaction fee on each sale, and payment processing fees of 4% + 20p per transaction. On a £20 sale, you keep roughly £16.60 after fees. These are higher than eBay for some items — factor them into your pricing before you list.

Etsy Ads can boost visibility but are optional and rarely worth it until you've established which listings convert organically.

The case for digital products

Digital products are Etsy's hidden gem for passive income. You create a file (a planner, template, print, SVG, spreadsheet, or document), upload it once, and Etsy delivers it automatically to every buyer. No stock, no postage, no handling — just ongoing sales from a listing you made once.

Popular UK digital products include budget planners, meal planners, CV templates, invoice templates, party printables, and wall art. See our Print on Demand guide for ideas on physical product designs.
