Dropshipping UK: The Honest Guide for 2026 — Is It Still Worth It?

Dropshipping is one of the most Googled side hustles in the UK — and one of the most misrepresented. This guide tells you what it actually is, what it actually earns, why most people fail, and what the better alternatives are.

The Second Income UK•10 min read•£0–£500/mo

What Dropshipping Actually Is

You create an online shop (typically on Shopify), list products you don't own, collect payment when someone orders, then forward the order to a supplier who ships directly to your customer. You keep the margin — the difference between what you charged and what the supplier charges you.

In theory: passive income with no stock needed. In reality: the margins are thin (5–20%), ads are expensive, suppliers are unreliable, and competition is intense.

The Honest Numbers

A realistic dropshipping store takes £500–£2,000 to set up properly (Shopify subscription, product samples, ad spend). Most beginners lose money in the first 1–3 months while learning paid advertising. Those who succeed typically earn £500–£3,000/month in profit — but it's genuinely difficult and takes 6–12 months to get there.

The success stories you see on social media are real — but they represent a small minority. According to industry data, most dropshipping stores fail within the first six months.

The Real Problems With Dropshipping in 2026

Ad costs: Facebook and Google ads — the main traffic source for dropshipping — have become significantly more expensive in recent years. Getting profitable requires real skill and ongoing investment.

Supplier reliability: Most dropshippers use AliExpress or similar platforms, with slow shipping times (2–4 weeks) and inconsistent quality. UK customers expect fast delivery and easy returns.

Competition: Any product that's working gets copied within weeks. Margins compress rapidly.

Better Alternatives to Dropshipping

If you want to sell products online without stock, print on demand has better margins and no ad spend required. If you want to sell things you don't own, affiliate marketing (linking to products and earning commission) is lower risk with no customer service headaches. If you want e-commerce specifically, Etsy digital products scale well without the risks of physical product selling.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is dropshipping legal in the UK?

Yes, dropshipping is completely legal in the UK. You are acting as a retailer who outsources fulfilment — this is a standard business model.

How much does it cost to start dropshipping in the UK?

Minimum realistic budget is £500–£1,000 (Shopify subscription, domain, initial ad spend). Most successful stores invest £2,000–£5,000 in the first year before becoming consistently profitable.

What is a more reliable alternative to dropshipping?

Print on demand (Printful/Redbubble) is lower risk with no ad spend needed. Selling digital products on Etsy is genuinely passive once set up. Both have lower earning ceilings but much higher success rates.
