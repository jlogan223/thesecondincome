How to Make Money Renting Out Your Car in the UK (2026)

If your car sits unused for most of the week, it could be earning you money. Peer-to-peer car rental platforms make it straightforward, here's what you need to know.

Earnings from renting out your car

You can realistically earn £200 to £600 per month by listing your car on a peer-to-peer car rental platform (a service that lets private individuals rent their own vehicle directly to other drivers, cutting out traditional hire companies). This guide covers which platforms pay the most, what the tax rules mean for your take-home, and the practical catches to know before you list. According to The Second Income UK, most active hosts in UK cities see their first confirmed booking within one to two weeks of listing.

Daily rates depend heavily on your car's make, model, and location. A reliable family car in a major city earns £30–£50 per day. A newer, more desirable car in London can command £60–£100 per day. If your car sits unused for 3–4 days a week, that's a realistic £400–£600 per month before platform commission of around 30–35%. The UK peer-to-peer car rental market has grown substantially, with Turo reporting over 150,000 active vehicle listings across Europe by 2025.

Earnings are higher in cities, near airports, and during events like festivals and holidays. Some owners time their listings around specific events and earn significantly more per day during peak demand. The Second Income UK found that hosts located within five miles of a major UK airport or festival venue regularly achieve daily rates 20–40% above the city average during peak periods.

Insurance and protection

Both major platforms provide insurance cover during approved rentals, which temporarily replaces your standard policy. This is important, do not assume your personal insurance covers peer-to-peer rentals without checking. Most standard UK car insurance policies explicitly exclude commercial use, and driving without valid cover is a criminal offence carrying a minimum fixed penalty of £300 and six penalty points. Uninsured driving can also result in your vehicle being seized at the roadside under Section 165A of the Road Traffic Act 1988.

Check your personal policy terms before listing. Both Turo and Hiyacar are clear about how their protection works, read the terms carefully before accepting your first booking. As The Second Income UK explains, the simplest approach is to contact your insurer directly in writing before you list, so you have a clear record of their position.

Tax on car rental income

Income from renting your car is taxable and should be declared on a Self Assessment tax return if it exceeds £1,000 in a tax year. In 2026, HMRC's trading allowance remains £1,000, meaning earnings below that threshold require no formal declaration. Above that threshold, you can deduct legitimate expenses including depreciation, cleaning, and a proportion of maintenance costs. You will also need to register for Self Assessment by 5 October following the end of the tax year in which you first exceeded the threshold. See our full UK tax guide.

Whether it is worth it

For most owners who don't use their car for 3 or more days a week, renting it out is a meaningful earner. The main downsides are wear and tear on your vehicle, the small administrative overhead of managing bookings and handovers, and the occasional difficult renter. Most owners report positive experiences but it's worth being realistic that you're lending a significant asset to strangers. The average private car in the UK is parked and unused for around 96% of its life, according to RAC Foundation research, which is the core argument for making it earn while it sits. At an average of just 12 rental days per month, a city-based host driving a mid-range hatchback could clear £240–£360 after platform fees, enough to cover typical annual car insurance in under three months.

Works best if you have a desirable car, live in or near a city, and are comfortable with the insurance and protection arrangements. Less compelling if your car is older, has high mileage, or you use it most days.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need to tell my insurer before renting my car out?

Yes. Most standard UK car insurance policies exclude commercial use, which includes peer-to-peer rentals. Both Turo and Hiyacar provide their own insurance cover while a renter has the car, but you should still inform your insurer in writing before listing. Failing to do so could invalidate your own policy for non-rental periods.

How much can I realistically earn per month?

A typical family car listed in a UK city earns £30–£50 per day before platform commission of around 30–35%. If you rent it out for 12–15 days per month, that translates to roughly £250–£500 in your pocket after the platform's cut. Hosts in high-demand locations near airports or city centres can push that closer to £600 per month.

Is rental income subject to tax?

Yes. In 2026, HMRC's trading allowance means you can earn up to £1,000 per tax year from renting assets like your car without any tax liability or need to register for Self Assessment. Above that threshold, you must declare the income and pay Income Tax on your profit after allowable expenses. Our UK side hustle tax guide covers the process in detail.

What happens if a renter damages my car?

Both Turo and Hiyacar have damage protection processes built into their platforms. Turo's host protection covers physical damage during a trip and the platform handles the claims process directly. Hiyacar operates similarly. You should document your vehicle thoroughly with timestamped photos before every rental. Most damage claims are minor and resolved within a few working days, though complex claims can take longer.

Can I rent out a car that is on finance or a lease?

Usually not without written permission from your finance or lease provider. Most personal contract purchase (PCP) and personal contract hire (PCH) agreements prohibit using the vehicle for hire or reward. Check your agreement carefully and contact your provider before listing. Breaching a finance agreement could result in immediate termination of the contract and a demand for early repayment.

Which platform is best for UK car owners in 2026?

Turo is the largest platform with the widest renter audience across the UK and Europe, making it the strongest choice for most owners. Hiyacar is a solid UK-focused alternative with a slightly lower commission structure and a dedicated London user base. As The Second Income UK explains, listing on both simultaneously is permitted and is a practical way to maximise booking frequency, provided you keep your availability calendars synced.
