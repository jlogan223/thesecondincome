TopCashback UK Review 2026: Is It Worth Signing Up?

TopCashback is one of the UK's most popular cashback sites, and for good reason. If you shop online regularly, signing up to topcashback.co.uk could put a few hundred pounds back in your pocket each year without changing where you shop or what you buy.

The Second Income UK • 8 min read • £100–£500/yr

What TopCashback is and how it works

TopCashback is a cashback website that pays you a percentage of your spending when you click through to a retailer via their site and then make a purchase. It sounds almost too simple, but the mechanics behind it are straightforward enough. Retailers pay TopCashback a commission for sending them a customer, and TopCashback passes most of that commission back to you as cashback.

The site launched in 2005 and has grown to become one of the largest cashback platforms in the UK, with over 20 million members and partnerships with thousands of retailers including Argos, Booking.com, Sky, and most of the major supermarkets and insurance providers. It consistently claims to offer the highest cashback rates of any UK cashback site, though rates vary by retailer and change frequently.

Cashback rates are listed as either a flat amount per transaction or as a percentage of what you spend. Some of the best rates come from financial products like credit cards, broadband deals, and insurance policies, where a single transaction can earn you £50 or more. Everyday shopping at clothing or electronics retailers tends to earn smaller percentages, typically between 1% and 10%.

How to sign up and create an account

Signing up to TopCashback is free and takes about two minutes. You visit topcashback.co.uk, click the sign-up button, and provide your email address and a password. There's no need to give bank details upfront. You can browse available cashback rates before you've even created an account, which is handy for checking whether a purchase you're planning is covered.

Once registered, it's worth taking a few minutes to fill out your profile and set up a payment method in advance. TopCashback pays out via bank transfer, PayPal, or as gift cards. Bank transfer is the most flexible option for most people. You'll also be asked whether you want a free account or whether you'd like to upgrade to TopCashback Plus, which is their paid membership tier (more on that below).

The mobile app is available on both iOS and Android and is worth downloading if you want quick access to cashback codes or to check rates while you're out and about. It works just as well as the desktop site for most purposes.

How to earn and track cashback

The golden rule with TopCashback is simple: always start your shopping journey from the TopCashback website or app, rather than going directly to the retailer. If you go directly to the retailer's site and then try to claim cashback afterwards, it won't work. You need to click through from TopCashback so the tracking link can register your visit.

Once you've clicked through and completed your purchase, cashback typically appears in your TopCashback account within a few hours, though sometimes it can take a day or two. At this point it's marked as "Payable pending," which means the retailer still needs to confirm the sale before TopCashback releases the funds. This confirmation stage is where most of the waiting happens.

You can track all your pending and confirmed cashback from your account dashboard. The dashboard shows each transaction, when it was tracked, and what stage it's at. It's worth checking this periodically to make sure your purchases have tracked correctly, especially for larger amounts.

A few things can prevent cashback from tracking properly. Using ad-blockers, VPNs, or certain browser extensions can interfere with the tracking cookie. Clearing your cookies between clicking through and completing your purchase can also cause issues. If a transaction hasn't tracked within a week or so, you can raise a claim through TopCashback's support system.

How and when TopCashback pays out

Cashback goes through two stages before you can withdraw it. First it shows as "Payable pending," then it moves to "Payable," which means it's ready to withdraw. How long this takes depends entirely on the retailer. Some retailers confirm sales within a few days; others, particularly insurance or financial products, can take several months because they want to make sure you haven't cancelled the policy or returned the item.

Once your cashback is marked as Payable, you can request a withdrawal at any time. There's no minimum withdrawal threshold for bank transfers, which is a genuine advantage over some competitors. PayPal withdrawals are also available, and gift card payouts sometimes come with a small bonus on top of your cashback value, making them worth considering if there's a retailer you use regularly.

Withdrawals to your bank account typically arrive within three to five working days. There are no fees for withdrawing on the free account tier.

TopCashback Plus membership: is it worth it

TopCashback Plus costs £5 per month (or £60 per year if you pay annually). In exchange, you get higher cashback rates on selected retailers, typically around 5% more than the standard rate, along with a few perks like priority customer service and access to exclusive cashback offers.

Whether it's worth it depends entirely on how much you spend through the platform. If you're an occasional user who only earns £50 or £100 a year in cashback, paying £60 for Plus doesn't make much financial sense. But if you're a heavy user who routes significant spending through TopCashback, including insurance renewals, utility switches, and regular online shopping, the enhanced rates could easily cover the membership cost and then some.

One thing worth noting: TopCashback sometimes offers a free trial of Plus, so it's worth checking whether a trial is available when you sign up. You can always cancel before the trial ends if you decide it's not for you.

Pros and cons compared with Quidco

TopCashback and Quidco are the two dominant cashback sites in the UK, and most serious cashback users have accounts with both. That said, they're not identical, and it's useful to understand where each tends to be stronger. For a detailed side-by-side breakdown, have a look at our TopCashback vs Quidco comparison.

The headline difference is cashback rates. TopCashback generally advertises slightly higher rates on more retailers, and it has a larger network overall. Quidco, on the other hand, sometimes has better rates on specific categories like travel or grocery cashback, so it's always worth comparing both before a big purchase.

Quidco charges a £1 per month membership fee on its free tier (deducted from your cashback earnings), while TopCashback's free tier is genuinely free. That gives TopCashback a slight edge for casual users who aren't spending enough to offset Quidco's fee.

Both platforms have their quirks when it comes to tracking reliability and customer service. Neither is perfect, and both occasionally miss transactions. Having accounts on both and comparing rates before each purchase is the most practical approach.

Tips to maximise your cashback earnings

The most consistent way to earn meaningful cashback is to remember to use TopCashback before any significant online purchase. Insurance renewals, broadband switches, and new phone or energy contracts are particularly valuable because the cashback on financial products tends to be high, sometimes well over £50 per transaction.

Stack cashback with other offers where you can. Using a cashback credit card in addition to clicking through TopCashback can effectively double your reward on some purchases, as long as you pay the card off in full each month to avoid interest charges eating into your gains.

Keep an eye on the "Featured Deals" and "Increased Rates" sections of the site. TopCashback regularly runs promotions where rates are temporarily boosted, and these can be significantly better than the standard rate. Setting up email alerts for your favourite retailers is a good way to catch these.

Finally, cashback is a genuinely useful passive income stream when combined with other approaches. If you're looking for broader ways to make money from home, cashback sites are one of the lower-effort options available, since you're earning on spending you'd be doing anyway.

Common problems and how to resolve them

The most common issue is cashback not tracking. If this happens, first check that you didn't have an ad-blocker or VPN active during the session, and that you completed the purchase in the same browser session as the click-through without clearing cookies in between. If everything looks correct on your end, submit a missing cashback claim through TopCashback's support portal. You'll need proof of purchase, such as a confirmation email, and the retailer's order number.

Claims can take time to resolve, sometimes several weeks, so patience is required. TopCashback does generally resolve legitimate claims in the member's favour, but the process isn't instant. For disputed amounts over £20 or so, it's worth persisting.

Another common point of confusion is the difference between "Payable pending" and "Payable" status. Cashback sitting at "Payable pending" can't be withdrawn yet, and there's not much you can do to speed up the retailer's confirmation process. Most pending cashback does eventually confirm, but it can take anywhere from a few weeks to several months for certain product categories.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is TopCashback legit and safe to use?

Yes, TopCashback is a legitimate UK company that has been operating since 2005. It is used by millions of people in the UK and is regulated under standard UK consumer and data protection laws. You don't need to share payment card details with TopCashback to earn cashback; you're simply clicking through their site before shopping as normal.

How much can you realistically earn from TopCashback each year?

Most regular users earn somewhere between £100 and £500 per year, though this varies widely depending on how much you shop online and whether you use the site for high-value transactions like insurance or utility switches. Occasional users who only shop at a handful of retailers might earn £20 to £50, while heavy users who route most of their online spending through the platform can earn more.

Do I need to pay tax on TopCashback earnings?

HMRC generally treats cashback as a discount on a purchase rather than income, so in most cases it is not taxable. However, if you are earning cashback through a business account or as part of a commercial arrangement, you should check with an accountant. For personal shopping cashback on a standard account, there is typically no tax to worry about.

Why hasn't my cashback tracked?

The most likely causes are an active ad-blocker or VPN, cookies being cleared between clicking the link and completing the purchase, or using a different browser or device. If you believe tracking failed despite doing everything correctly, raise a missing cashback claim through your TopCashback account and provide your order confirmation as evidence.

How long does it take for TopCashback to pay out?

Once your cashback reaches "Payable" status you can request a withdrawal at any time, and bank transfers typically arrive within three to five working days. The wait to reach Payable status depends on the retailer; it can be as short as a few days for some purchases or as long as several months for insurance policies and financial products.

Should I use TopCashback or Quidco?

Ideally both, since each platform occasionally offers better rates on different retailers. If you only want one account, TopCashback is generally the stronger choice for most users because its free tier has no membership fee and it tends to have a larger range of retailers with competitive rates. That said, always compare rates before a significant purchase, as Quidco sometimes comes out ahead on specific categories.
