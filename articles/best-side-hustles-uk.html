Best Side Hustles UK 2026: 20 Ideas That Actually Pay

Side hustles in the UK are booming, over 39% of Brits now earn money outside their main job, averaging £470 extra per month. But most side hustle content is either American or full of hype. This guide covers only UK-viable, realistic options with honest earning estimates.

The Second Income UK • 14 min read • £100–£2,000+/mo

What Makes a Good Side Hustle?

Before diving into the list, it helps to understand what separates a genuinely useful side hustle from a waste of time. The best ones have low upfront costs, fit around a full-time job, pay a decent hourly rate, and have a clear path to getting started quickly. This list is filtered by those criteria, no dropshipping fantasies, no "post on TikTok and get rich" fluff.

The other thing worth saying upfront: most side hustles require real effort in the early weeks. The ones that become genuinely passive, cashback, renting out assets, investing, still take setup time. Any site telling you £1,000/month with zero effort is misleading you.

Highest Earning Side Hustles UK

1. Matched Betting, £500–£1,500/month
Arguably the most reliable high-earning side hustle available to UK adults. Uses free bet promotions from bookmakers to guarantee a profit regardless of outcome. Legal, tax-free, and well-documented. Hardest part is the initial learning curve, most people earn £200–£500 in their first month working through welcome offers. Read our full matched betting guide.

2. Freelancing, £200–£2,000+/month
Writing, design, web development, video editing, bookkeeping, social media management, if you have a skill used in an office, someone will pay for it freelance. UK platforms like Fiverr and PeoplePerHour make it relatively easy to get started without a portfolio. Read our freelancing for beginners guide.

3. Online Tutoring, £20–£50/hour
One of the most consistently recommended UK side hustles. Strong demand for GCSE and A-level maths, English and sciences. Platforms like Tutorful, MyTutor, and Superprof handle payment and finding students. You set your own rate and hours.

4. Selling on eBay or Vinted, £100–£1,200/month
Clearing out unwanted items is the starting point, but many UK sellers build real income by sourcing items from car boot sales, charity shops and auctions. Vinted is now the dominant platform for clothing with zero seller fees. Read our eBay selling guide.

Mid-Range Side Hustles (£100–£500/month)

5. Paid Surveys, £30–£150/month
Lower earner but genuinely passive once set up. Best UK survey sites are Prolific (highest pay, waitlist), Swagbucks, and Survey Junkie. Won't replace your salary but covers a few bills. See our ranked survey site guide.

6. Cashback Apps, £100–£400/year
Not a side hustle in the traditional sense but genuinely free money on purchases you'd make anyway. TopCashback and Quidco are the two main UK platforms. Compare the two here.

7. Print on Demand, £50–£500/month
Create designs once, sell them on T-shirts, mugs and phone cases without holding stock. Redbubble and Printful are the main UK-friendly platforms. Income is slow to build but genuinely passive once designs are ranking. Read our print on demand guide.

8. Etsy Shop, £100–£1,500/month
Digital products, printable planners, CV templates, wall art, sell repeatedly with no shipping. Physical handmade items work too but require more time. Read our Etsy guide.

9. Delivery Driving (Deliveroo/Uber Eats), £8–£14/hour
Flexible, quick to start, and genuinely pays well during peak hours. Works around any schedule. Requires a vehicle or bicycle and passing a background check.

10. Dog Walking / Pet Sitting, £100–£400/month
Always in demand in UK towns and cities. Rover and Pawshake connect walkers with pet owners. Builds quickly through word of mouth and repeat clients.

Asset-Based Side Hustles (Low Effort Once Set Up)

11. Rent Out Your Driveway, £50–£200/month
Completely passive. Apps like JustPark and YourParkingSpace let you list a driveway or parking space to rent by the day or month. Particularly valuable near train stations, hospitals and city centres. Read our driveway rental guide.

12. Rent Out Your Car, £150–£400/month
If your car sits unused while you're at work, Turo lets you rent it to vetted drivers with insurance included. Read our car rental guide.

13. Investing, £50–£500+/month (long term)
Not a side hustle in the short term, but putting even £50/month into a stocks and shares ISA builds meaningful passive income over years. Freetrade and Trading 212 make it easy to start with small amounts. Read our beginner investing guide.

14. Airbnb, £300–£1,500+/month
Spare room, holiday home, or even just your home while you're away. Airbnb hosts in the UK average £3,500/month with a full property, though a spare room more realistically earns £300–£600.

Digital and Creative Side Hustles

15. Sell Digital Products, £50–£800/month
Planners, templates, spreadsheets, guides, anything a professional creates can be sold repeatedly. Gumroad and Etsy are both good platforms. Passive once built.

16. Virtual Assistant, £15–£30/hour
Inbox management, scheduling, research, data entry. Businesses hire VAs remotely and the work fits easily around other commitments. Fiverr and Upwork are the main starting platforms.

17. Website Testing, £5–£15 per test
Companies pay for feedback on websites and apps. UserTesting, Maze and Testbirds all operate in the UK. Typically 15–20 minutes per test. Read our full guide.

18. Content Creation (YouTube/TikTok), Variable
Long game but high ceiling. Channels with 10,000+ engaged subscribers can earn through ads, affiliates and sponsorships. Realistically 12–18 months before meaningful income.

19. Stock Photography, £20–£200/month
Upload photos to Shutterstock, Adobe Stock or Alamy and earn each time they're downloaded. Best for people who already photograph regularly.

20. Become a Mystery Shopper, £50–£200/month
Visit shops, restaurants and venues to evaluate customer experience. UK firms include Grass Roots and Market Force. Pays modestly but requires very little skill or time.

Which Side Hustle Should You Start?

The honest answer: the best side hustle is the one that fits your existing skills and schedule. If you have a professional skill, freelancing gives the best hourly rate. If you want something truly passive, start with cashback and driveway rental, lowest effort, free money. If you have a few hours per week and want to build something meaningful, matched betting offers the most consistent returns for time invested in the UK.

The biggest mistake most people make is starting three things at once and doing none of them properly. Pick one, give it four weeks of genuine effort, and see where it goes.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best side hustle in the UK in 2026?

Matched betting offers the most consistent returns for time invested, most beginners earn £500–£1,500 in their first month. For long-term passive income, investing and renting out assets are the top picks.

Do I need to pay tax on my side hustle?

If you earn more than £1,000 per year from a side hustle, you need to register for Self Assessment with HMRC. The first £1,000 is covered by the Trading Allowance and is tax-free.

How much can you realistically earn from a side hustle in the UK?

According to Monzo's 2026 research, the average UK side hustler earns £470 per month. Most people (68%) earn less than £500/month, but for many that's enough to cover significant bills or build savings.

Can I do a side hustle while employed full time?

Yes. Most side hustles are completely compatible with full-time employment. Check your employment contract for any non-compete clauses, but in most cases there is no restriction on earning money outside your main job.
