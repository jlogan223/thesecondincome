What are cashback sites, and how much can you earn?

The short answer: using TopCashback and Quidco together, most UK shoppers can realistically earn £150 to £400 per year on purchases they would have made anyway. Cashback sites are free services that pay you a percentage of your spend every time you shop through their links. They work by receiving a commission from retailers and passing most of it back to you as cash. When you click through to a retailer via TopCashback or Quidco and complete a purchase, the retailer pays the cashback site a referral fee, typically ranging from 1% to 15% of the transaction value, and the site returns the majority of that fee to your account. According to The Second Income UK, members who check both platforms before every purchase consistently come out ahead of those who rely on just one site, often by £30 to £60 per year on the same spending habits.

It costs you nothing extra and takes about 10 seconds to set up per site. In 2026, both TopCashback and Quidco have expanded their retailer networks significantly, with combined coverage now exceeding 9,000 UK and international merchants across categories from supermarkets to insurance providers.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is TopCashback genuinely free to use?

Yes. TopCashback's standard plan has no monthly fee and passes 100% of the retailer commission back to you. There is an optional paid tier called TopCashback Plus at £5 per month, which offers boosted rates on selected retailers, but the free plan is sufficient for most users.

Does Quidco charge a fee?

Quidco's free plan deducts £1 per month directly from your cashback balance, which works out at £12 per year. If your annual cashback earnings are below £12, you will receive nothing. For most regular shoppers this threshold is easily cleared, but light users may prefer TopCashback. Quidco also offers a Premium plan at £5 per month with higher rates on many retailers.

How long does cashback take to pay out?

Cashback on both TopCashback and Quidco typically takes between 7 days and 3 months to confirm, depending on the retailer. Fast-tracking cashback to your account sooner is available for a small fee on both platforms. Once confirmed, payouts to your bank account, PayPal, or gift cards are usually processed within 1 to 2 working days.

Can cashback earnings affect my tax position?

HMRC generally treats cashback as a discount rather than income, so in most cases it is not taxable. However, if you earn cashback through a business account or as part of a trading activity, different rules may apply. As of 2026, HMRC's trading allowance remains £1,000 per year for miscellaneous income, and straightforward personal cashback falls well below any threshold that would trigger a tax liability for most people. If you are unsure, consult a qualified accountant.

What happens if my cashback does not track?

Both TopCashback and Quidco have a claims process for missing cashback. You will typically need to submit proof of purchase, such as a confirmation email, within 30 to 60 days of the transaction. Success rates for valid claims are generally high, but cashback can fail to track if you use browser extensions that block cookies, switch devices mid-purchase, or apply a discount code not listed on the cashback site.

Which platform is better for mobile shopping?

Quidco's app receives consistently higher ratings on both the App Store and Google Play, with an average score of around 4.5 out of 5 as of early 2026. TopCashback's app is functional but considered less intuitive by many users. For desktop shopping, both platforms are broadly comparable. If you do most of your shopping on a smartphone, Quidco's app experience gives it a meaningful advantage.
