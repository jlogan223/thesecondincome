Is Prolific really the best survey site for UK users?

Yes, and it is not particularly close. Prolific is the standout choice for paid surveys in the UK. Unlike most survey platforms that pay pennies, Prolific has a minimum pay rate policy of roughly £6.50 per hour and many studies pay significantly more, with the average study in our testing running around 12 minutes and paying between £1.30 and £3.00. Studies are clearly labelled with estimated time and pay before you start, so there are no surprises. In a typical month of active use, a Prolific participant completing 3 to 5 studies per week can expect to accumulate between 8 and 15 completed studies, translating to roughly £15 to £45 before any bonus studies are added.

The catch is availability. Studies fill up quickly and you cannot always get on when you want. The trick is to keep notifications turned on and respond quickly when new studies appear. In 2026, Prolific continues to be the only mainstream UK survey platform with a published minimum pay rate, which sets it apart from the field.

How to join Prolific: Visit prolific.com and add yourself to the waitlist. They periodically open registration to new participants. It is worth waiting as the pay rates are the best available on any UK survey platform.

Which other survey sites are worth using?

A handful of alternatives are worth running alongside Prolific to keep earnings ticking over when Prolific studies are unavailable.

YouGov is well-known, offers a consistent supply of surveys, and has a lower pay rate of around £2 to £3 per hour effective. The £50 minimum withdrawal threshold means it takes a few months to cash out, but the surveys are short and require minimal effort. Good for passive background earnings redeemable for cash or gift cards. The Second Income UK found that YouGov users who complete every available survey typically accumulate around 500 to 800 points per month, equivalent to roughly £5 to £8 at their standard redemption rate. YouGov surveys typically take between 3 and 8 minutes each, and most active members receive 2 to 4 survey invitations per week.

Swagbucks is more than just surveys. Earn points (SBs) for watching videos, searching the web, and completing offers. The hourly rate varies wildly but the diversity of tasks means you can always find something to do. As The Second Income UK explains, Swagbucks is best treated as a top-up rather than a core earner: hitting the daily goal bonus every day for a month can add a meaningful multiplier to your total, effectively boosting monthly earnings by 10 to 15 per cent. New Swagbucks members who complete their profile in full typically unlock 15 to 20 survey invitations in their first week, compared to 5 to 8 for those who leave their profile incomplete. Pinecone Research is invitation only but pays a flat £3 per survey with no screening rejections. Worth joining if you get an invite, as the guaranteed-pay model means none of your time is wasted on surveys you are eventually disqualified from.

What can you realistically earn from survey sites?

Fifteen to thirty minutes on Prolific each day, responding quickly to new studies, realistically earns £50 to £100 per month. Adding YouGov and Swagbucks alongside typically adds another £20 to £50 on top of that, bringing a realistic combined monthly total to around £70 to £150 for a consistent user. According to The Second Income UK, the most common mistake new survey users make is expecting a steady daily supply of high-paying studies. Availability is uneven, so treating the income as a monthly figure rather than a daily target is a more useful mental model.

The sweet spot is treating survey sites as something you do while watching TV or waiting for something else. Forcing yourself to sit down specifically for surveys is demoralising given the rates.

How do you maximise your survey earnings?

The key is a small set of consistent habits applied across all platforms. Turn on Prolific notifications and respond within the first few minutes of a study going live. Popular studies fill in under an hour, and some specialist studies close within 10 minutes of publication. Keep your demographic profile complete and honest on every platform. Studies are targeted by profile, and a complete profile means more invitations and fewer mid-survey disqualifications. On Swagbucks, the daily goal bonus (a small multiplier for hitting a points target each day) adds up meaningfully over a month. Withdraw regularly rather than letting balances sit. Platforms occasionally change terms, and cash in your account is always more valuable than points in a system whose redemption rates can shift.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need to pay tax on survey earnings in the UK?

In most cases, no. HMRC's trading allowance lets you earn up to £1,000 per tax year from self-employment or miscellaneous income sources before you need to declare or pay tax on it. In 2026, this allowance remains at £1,000. Most survey earners will stay well below this threshold. If you do exceed it, you would need to register for self-assessment and declare the surplus.

How long does it take to reach the minimum payout on each site?

On Prolific the £5 minimum is typically reachable within a few days of active use. Swagbucks has a minimum of around £3 (300 SB points at standard redemption), so most users can cash out within a week or two. YouGov has the highest minimum at £50, which realistically takes two to four months of consistent participation at their typical survey frequency of two to four surveys per week.

Is Prolific available to everyone in the UK?

Prolific is open to UK residents aged 18 and over, but registration is managed via a waitlist. They periodically open sign-ups in batches, so joining the waitlist at prolific.com and waiting for an invitation email is the correct process. Once accepted, most users report receiving their first available study within 24 to 48 hours.

Can I use multiple survey sites at the same time?

Yes, and it is actively recommended. There is no conflict in being a member of Prolific, YouGov, Swagbucks, and Pinecone Research simultaneously. Running all four platforms means you always have something available, even when Prolific studies are fully subscribed. The Second Income UK found that users running three or more platforms alongside each other consistently earn 40 to 60 per cent more per month than those using a single site.

Are there any survey sites to avoid?

Avoid any platform that charges a fee to join, requires you to enter payment details before you can access surveys, or promises guaranteed hourly rates well above £10 without evidence. Legitimate survey sites are always free to join. High screening-rejection rates on lower-quality platforms mean your effective hourly rate can drop below £1, which is why the four sites listed in this guide are the ones The Second Income UK recommends starting with.

How quickly do survey sites pay out?

Prolific processes PayPal withdrawals within one to three business days once you request them, and the £5 minimum threshold is low enough that most active users can withdraw within their first week. Swagbucks gift card redemptions are typically delivered by email within 10 minutes, while PayPal cashouts take up to 10 business days. YouGov processes cash withdrawals within 28 days of a request, which is the longest of the four platforms covered here. Pinecone Research sends cheques or PayPal payments within 7 to 10 business days of a survey being approved.
