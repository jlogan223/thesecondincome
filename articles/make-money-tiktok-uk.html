How to Make Money on TikTok in the UK in 2026

UK creators can realistically earn anywhere from £50 to over £2,000 per month on TikTok, depending on their audience size and which income streams they use. The route to that income, however, is more nuanced than the viral success stories suggest. This guide covers exactly how UK creators make money on TikTok (the practice of earning income from short-form video content and the tools TikTok provides to creators), what it realistically takes, and which methods are worth prioritising.

The Second Income UK•9 min read•£50–£2,000+/mo

What Are the Four Ways to Earn Money on TikTok?

There are four main income streams available to UK creators in 2026, and they vary significantly in how much effort they require relative to what they pay.

1. TikTok Creator Rewards Program
TikTok pays creators based on video performance. To qualify you need at least 10,000 followers and 100,000 views in the last 30 days. Pay rates vary but typically sit at £0.02–£0.04 per 1,000 views, meaning a video with 100,000 views earns around £2–£4. This alone won't make you rich. As The Second Income UK explains, the Creator Rewards Program is best treated as a small bonus on top of other income streams rather than a primary earner.

2. TikTok LIVE Gifts
During live streams, viewers can send virtual gifts that convert to real money. Some UK creators earn £50–£500+ per live session once they have an engaged audience. Requires 1,000+ followers to go live. According to The Second Income UK, creators who go live consistently at least three times per week tend to see significantly higher gift income than those who stream occasionally.

3. Brand Deals and Sponsored Content
This is where real TikTok income comes from. UK creators with 10,000–50,000 followers can charge £100–£500 per sponsored post. Those with 100,000+ followers typically earn £500–£5,000 per brand deal. Platforms like Creator.co and Influencer.com connect smaller creators with brands.

4. TikTok Shop Affiliate
Promote products from TikTok Shop in your videos and earn 5–20% commission on each sale. This has become the fastest-growing income stream for UK TikTok creators in 2026. You don't need a huge audience, just content that converts. The Second Income UK found that TikTok Shop affiliate is the single most accessible income stream for creators with fewer than 10,000 followers, since commissions can be earned from the very first sale regardless of overall audience size.

What Do You Actually Need to Start Earning?

Most creators don't earn meaningful income until 6–12 months in. The exception is TikTok Shop affiliate, where a well-placed product review with as few as 5,000 views can generate £50–£200 in commission within days. In 2026, HMRC's trading allowance remains £1,000 per tax year, meaning most beginners can earn their first TikTok income without registering as self-employed straight away.

The niches that monetise best in the UK: personal finance (ironically), cleaning and home organisation, fashion and beauty, cooking, and small business and entrepreneurship content. Finance and home-lifestyle creators in particular attract brand deal rates at the higher end of the scale, with some micro-influencers in these niches charging upwards of £300 per sponsored video even at 20,000 followers.

Is TikTok Worth Starting as a Side Hustle?

The short answer is: it depends heavily on how you approach it. If you're already spending time on TikTok, starting to create content costs nothing and has real upside. If you're starting from scratch purely for money, it's a long game. Expect 6–18 months before meaningful income arrives. The creators who do best treat TikTok as a platform to build an audience that they then monetise through multiple channels including their own products, services, and affiliate links.

Frequently Asked Questions

How many followers do you need to make money on TikTok?

You can make money with very few followers through TikTok Shop affiliate, where a well-placed product review can earn commissions with any audience size. The Creator Rewards Program requires 10,000 followers minimum, and going live requires at least 1,000 followers.

How much does TikTok pay per view in the UK?

The Creator Rewards Program pays approximately £0.02–£0.04 per 1,000 views. A video with 1 million views might earn £20–£40. Brand deals and TikTok Shop affiliate are far more lucrative for most creators.

What type of TikTok content makes the most money in the UK?

TikTok Shop affiliate content such as product reviews and demonstrations earns the most relative to audience size. Finance, beauty, food, and home and lifestyle niches attract the most brand deal budgets.

Do I need to tell HMRC about TikTok income?

Yes, if your total TikTok earnings exceed £1,000 in a tax year you will need to register as self-employed and complete a Self Assessment tax return. In 2026, HMRC's £1,000 trading allowance covers most beginners, but once you exceed that threshold you must declare the income. Keep records of all payments received, including gift conversions and affiliate commissions.

Can you make money on TikTok without showing your face?

Yes. Many UK creators in niches such as cooking, home organisation, and product reviews build substantial audiences without appearing on camera. TikTok Shop affiliate in particular works well for faceless content because the focus is on the product rather than the creator's personality.

How long does it take to start earning on TikTok?

Via TikTok Shop affiliate, some creators earn their first commission within the first week of posting. Via the Creator Rewards Program, you need to reach 10,000 followers and 100,000 views in a 30-day period first, which typically takes 3–12 months depending on posting frequency and niche. Brand deals generally come once you have a consistent track record, usually after 6 months or more of regular posting.
