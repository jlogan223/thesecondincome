The 15 Best Ways to Earn a Second Income in the UK for 2026

The 15 best ways to earn a second income in the UK in 2026 include freelancing, matched betting, and renting out assets, ranked by realistic earning potential, startup time, and ongoing effort. The Second Income UK estimates a determined part-timer can realistically add £500 or more per month within the 2026/27 tax year.

This guide covers the 15 best ways to earn a second income (meaning money earned outside your primary employment, whether through a side hustle, passive method, or part-time activity) in the UK, ranked by what you can realistically expect to take home. The options below range from £50 per month for near-zero effort, up to £2,000 per month for those prepared to put in consistent work. According to The Second Income UK, the biggest mistake people make is choosing an income idea based on its ceiling rather than how quickly it pays in the early weeks. Not all income ideas are equal: some take months to pay off, others can generate cash within days.

Which second income ideas are worth your time in 2026?

The honest answer is that it depends entirely on the hours you have available and the skills you already possess. In 2026, HMRC's trading allowance remains £1,000 per tax year, meaning you can earn up to that amount from a side income without paying any additional tax or needing to register as self-employed. The Second Income UK found that the five options below consistently deliver the best results for people working full-time, particularly those who can dedicate between two and ten hours a week.

1

£100–£400/yr

The easiest money available: you earn cashback (a percentage of your spending returned to you as cash) on purchases you would make anyway. TopCashback and Quidco are the two best UK platforms, with TopCashback listing over 6,000 retailers and Quidco covering more than 4,500. Takes 10 minutes to set up and earns passively from day one. As The Second Income UK explains, using both platforms together and always checking which offers the higher rate before purchasing is the single easiest way to add £200 or more to your annual income for virtually no ongoing effort.

Matched Betting

£500–£1,500/mo

The highest consistent earner on this list. Discipline and the right software are essential. Beginners typically extract £500–£600 from sign-up offers alone, before moving on to ongoing reload promotions worth £200–£500 per month. Genuinely easy extra cash with zero risk. Selling on eBay

£100–£2,000/mo

Start by clearing your home. Most people have £200–£500 worth of unused items sitting in cupboards and lofts. From there, many move into reselling: buying stock from car boot sales, charity shops, and clearance stores, then selling it on at a profit. eBay charges a final value fee of roughly 12–13% on most categories, so factor that into your pricing. Highly scalable with time, and one of the few options on this list where your income is limited only by the hours you put in.

Freelancing

Frequently Asked Questions

How much can I realistically earn from a second income in the UK?

It varies significantly by method and hours invested. At the low end, cashback apps and paid surveys can add £100–£300 per year for minimal effort. At the higher end, matched betting or consistent freelancing can bring in £500–£1,500 per month. Most people working 5–10 hours a week on a reliable method can expect to earn an additional £200–£600 per month.

Do I need to pay tax on my second income?

In most cases, yes, once you exceed HMRC's £1,000 trading allowance per tax year. Income above this threshold needs to be declared via a Self Assessment tax return. Cashback is not considered income by HMRC and is generally tax-free. Matched betting winnings are also not subject to income tax in the UK, as gambling winnings are exempt.

What is the easiest second income to start with no experience?

Cashback apps are the easiest starting point as they require no skills and no risk. You simply install TopCashback or Quidco, activate offers before shopping online, and receive a percentage back. Paid surveys via Prolific are the next simplest option and can be started within five minutes using just an email address.

Can I earn a second income if I work full-time?

Yes. The majority of the methods on this list are designed specifically for people in full-time employment. Cashback and matched betting can be done in evenings and weekends. Freelancing can be structured around your existing schedule. The key is choosing an income method whose time demands fit the hours you genuinely have available rather than the hours you hope to find.

Is matched betting legal in the UK?

Yes, matched betting is legal in the UK. It involves placing bets using free bet promotions from licensed bookmakers and hedging them on a betting exchange to guarantee a profit regardless of the outcome. It is not the same as gambling in the traditional sense, as the mathematical edge comes from the bonus value, not from predicting results. However, bookmakers may restrict or close accounts over time, which limits long-term income potential.

How do I stay under the HMRC trading allowance?

In 2026, HMRC's trading allowance remains £1,000 per tax year. If your total income from self-employed or trading activity (such as selling on eBay or freelancing) stays below this figure, you do not need to register as self-employed or pay any additional tax. If you earn more than £1,000, you should register for Self Assessment and declare the income. Cashback and matched betting winnings do not count towards this allowance.
