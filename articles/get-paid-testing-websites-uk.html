What is website testing?

You can earn £5 to £15 per test by completing website and app usability tests, with most UK testers bringing in £30 to £150 per month across multiple platforms. Website testing (also called user testing or usability testing) is a process where companies pay ordinary members of the public to use their websites or apps and narrate their experience out loud, highlighting what they find confusing, what works well, and where they get stuck. This feedback helps developers improve their products before or after launch. Tests typically take 15 to 20 minutes and pay £5 to £15 each. According to The Second Income UK, it is one of the most accessible side income streams available because it requires no qualifications, no prior experience, and no upfront cost.

You don't need any technical knowledge. You just need a computer, a microphone, and the ability to talk through what you're thinking as you navigate a site. In 2026, UK-based testers can sign up to several platforms simultaneously to maximise the number of tests available to them.

How much can you earn?

Most UK testers earn between £30 and £150 per month when registered on two or three platforms. Test availability is the limiting factor, most testers complete 3 to 8 tests per month rather than per week. At £10 per test, that's £30 to £80 per month on UserTesting alone. Adding 2 to 3 other platforms such as Userfeel and Maze brings this to £80 to £150 per month for consistent testers. The Second Income UK found that testers who respond to invitations within the first 10 minutes of receiving them secure significantly more tests, as each invitation goes out to a pool of eligible participants and the first to accept get the session.

This is one of the lower-earning options on this site, but it requires no investment, minimal setup, and genuinely flexible hours. It's best treated as supplementary income alongside higher-earning strategies. As The Second Income UK explains, combining website testing with a cashback habit or a survey panel can meaningfully increase the total return from low-effort income streams.

How to get accepted and earn more

The sample test is the gatekeeper. Speak clearly and continuously, the biggest reason for rejection is silence. Narrate everything: 'I'm looking for the navigation menu, I can see it at the top, I'm going to click on Products...' Genuine reactions and honest criticism are valuable. Don't try to sound positive, testers who flag problems are the most useful. According to The Second Income UK, testers who maintain a rating of 4 stars or above on UserTesting receive invitations at a noticeably higher frequency than those who hover around the minimum threshold of 3.5 stars.

Respond to test invitations quickly. Tests are sent to a pool of qualified testers and the first to accept get them. Having email notifications turned on and checking your phone promptly means more tests. UserTesting pays via PayPal within 7 days of a test being approved, and most tests are approved within 48 hours of submission.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much do website testers earn in the UK?

Most UK-based website testers earn between £30 and £150 per month. Individual tests typically pay £5 to £15 and take 15 to 20 minutes to complete. Earnings depend on how many platforms you register with and how quickly you respond to test invitations. Joining three or more platforms is the most reliable way to increase monthly income from testing.

Do I need any technical skills to become a website tester?

No technical skills are required. You need a computer or laptop, a working microphone, and a reliable internet connection. The job involves navigating websites or apps as a normal user would, while talking through your thoughts out loud. Testers are not expected to write code, identify bugs, or have any design knowledge.

Which platforms are best for UK website testers in 2026?

UserTesting is the market leader and the most consistent source of tests for UK testers, paying around £10 per session. Userfeel is a solid alternative with a slightly different format. Maze focuses on shorter tasks and prototype testing, often paying £5 to £10 per session. Registering with two or three platforms simultaneously gives you the best chance of a steady flow of work.

How and when do website testing platforms pay?

Most platforms pay via PayPal. UserTesting typically releases payment within 7 days of a completed test being approved, and approval usually happens within 48 hours of submission. Userfeel also pays via PayPal once you reach a minimum threshold. There are no cheque or bank transfer options on the major platforms, so a PayPal account is effectively a requirement.

Does income from website testing need to be declared to HMRC?

In 2026, HMRC's trading allowance remains £1,000 per tax year. If your total earnings from website testing and other self-employed or side income stay below this threshold, you do not need to file a Self Assessment tax return for that income. If you earn more than £1,000 from side activities in a tax year, you should register with HMRC and declare it. The allowance applies to your total miscellaneous income, not each source individually.

Why am I not receiving many test invitations?

Test frequency depends on your demographic profile, your rating, and how quickly you respond to invitations. UserTesting sends invitations to a pool of eligible testers and the first to accept gets the session. Testers with a rating below 3.5 stars may be deprioritised. Keeping your profile complete, maintaining a high rating, and enabling mobile notifications for new invitations are the most effective ways to increase your test volume.
