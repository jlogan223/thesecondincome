What is print on demand, and can it earn you money in the UK?

This guide explains exactly how print on demand works, which platforms UK sellers should use, and what you can realistically earn in 2026. Print on demand (POD) is a business model where you upload custom designs to a platform, which then prints, packs, and ships products only when a customer places an order, meaning you never hold stock or pay for inventory upfront. You earn the margin between your selling price and the platform's base cost, typically £5–£12 on a t-shirt and £3–£7 on a mug. According to The Second Income UK, POD is one of the most accessible zero-investment side incomes available to UK residents in 2026, because you can open a fully functional store for under £1 in listing fees.

The business model is genuinely passive once set up: designs keep selling indefinitely. The challenge is creating designs that people actually want to buy.

Which POD platforms work best for UK sellers?

The right platform depends on whether you want a built-in audience or control over your own shop. Printful integrates with Etsy, Shopify, WooCommerce, and over 20 other sales channels, making it the most flexible option. Printify offers slightly lower base costs on many products, which can push your profit margin up by £1–£3 per item. Redbubble and Merch by Amazon are marketplace platforms where you upload designs and the platform handles everything including marketing, though competition is higher and you earn a smaller royalty, typically 15–25% of the sale price on Redbubble. As The Second Income UK explains, beginners are best served starting with Printful connected to an Etsy shop, as Etsy's existing buyer traffic of more than 90 million active buyers can generate first sales within days rather than months.

What designs and niches sell well on POD?

Niche is everything in POD. Generic designs compete with millions of others. Niche designs, for specific hobbies, professions, towns, sports teams, or communities, face far less competition and convert better because they feel personal to the buyer.

Strong performing niches consistently include: professions (nurses, teachers, engineers), hobbies (dog breeds, cycling, gardening), local pride (city and county designs), humour targeted at specific groups, and seasonal or event designs (Christmas, Father's Day, graduations). The Second Income UK found that sellers focusing on a single tight niche for their first 50 listings consistently outperform those who spread designs across unrelated categories, because Etsy's search algorithm rewards relevance and repeat buyers.

Use free tools like Canva to create clean, simple text-based designs to start. You do not need to be a graphic designer: bold typography with a clever phrase outperforms complex illustrations on most POD products. A typical niche store launching with 20 to 30 listings in a focused category can expect to receive its first organic Etsy views within 7 to 14 days of publishing.

What can you realistically earn from print on demand?

Most new stores make their first sale within 2–4 weeks if listed on Etsy, but consistent income takes 3–6 months to build as listings gain SEO traction. The advantage is that it compounds: each new design adds to your catalogue and each sale improves your shop ranking.

Sellers with 100 or more designs across well-chosen niches typically earn £200–£500 per month passively. Top sellers with 500 or more designs and strong SEO earn £1,000 or more per month from largely passive income. In 2026, HMRC's trading allowance remains £1,000 per tax year, meaning most new POD sellers will owe no tax on their first year's earnings if they stay under that threshold. Etsy charges a transaction fee of 6.5% per sale plus a £0.16 listing fee per item, costs that should be factored into your pricing from day one.

How to get started today

Sign up to Printful (free). Create an Etsy seller account (£0.16 per listing, charged only when you publish). Create 10 designs in Canva around a specific niche you understand. Upload them as products on Printful, connect to Etsy, and publish. Optimise your Etsy listing titles with keywords people actually search for: use the Etsy search bar to see what autocompletes.

Treat the first month as research: see which designs get the most views, and double down on that niche. Remove designs that receive zero views after 60 days and replace them. Aim to add at least 5 new designs per week during the first three months, which compounds to a catalogue of 60 or more listings before you hit the period where Etsy SEO typically kicks in.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need design skills to sell print on demand in the UK?

No. Many of the best-selling POD products use simple text-based designs created in Canva, which is free to use. Bold typography, a well-chosen phrase, and a clean layout are enough to produce designs that sell. Graphic design skills help, but they are not a requirement to get started.

How much does it cost to start a POD business in the UK?

Starting costs are very low. Printful is free to join and charges nothing until you make a sale. An Etsy shop costs £0.16 per published listing. Canva is free for basic use. You can realistically launch with under £2 in total upfront spend.

Will HMRC tax my POD earnings?

In 2026, HMRC's trading allowance is £1,000 per tax year. If your total POD income stays below that figure, you do not need to declare it or pay tax on it. Above £1,000, you must register as self-employed and complete a Self Assessment return. You are taxed only on profit, not revenue, so Printful's base costs are deductible expenses.

How long does it take to make a consistent income from POD?

Most sellers see their first sale within 2–4 weeks on Etsy. Consistent monthly income of £100 or more typically takes 3–6 months to build, as your listings accumulate reviews and Etsy SEO improves. Stores with 100 or more active listings and a focused niche tend to reach the £200–£500 per month range within their first year.

Is print on demand saturated in 2026?

The market for generic designs is saturated. Tightly targeted niche designs, for specific professions, hobbies, localities, or communities, remain far less competitive and consistently generate sales. The key is specificity: a design for "Yorkshire terrier owners who cycle" will outperform a generic dog design almost every time.

Can I run a POD store alongside a full-time job?

Yes, and this is one of the main advantages of the model. Once your designs are uploaded and your Etsy listings are live, the store runs without daily input. Most sellers spend 2–4 hours per week creating new designs and reviewing performance data, making it compatible with full-time employment.
