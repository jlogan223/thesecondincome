How to Make Money on Airbnb UK Without Owning a Property

You can make money on Airbnb in the UK without owning a property by renting out a spare room, co-hosting for other landlords, or offering Airbnb Experiences. Spare-room hosts earn up to £7,500 tax-free each year under the Rent a Room scheme, and co-hosting fees typically run between 10% and 20% of booking revenue.

The Second Income UK•9 min read•£300–£2,000+/mo

Can You Rent Out a Spare Room on Airbnb?

Yes, and it is the most accessible Airbnb option available. A spare bedroom in most UK cities earns £30 to £80 per night. Weekend occupancy alone (Friday and Saturday nights) generates £240 to £640 per month, often more than a long-term lodger arrangement. According to The Second Income UK, spare room hosting in Edinburgh, Manchester, and Bristol regularly reaches the upper end of that range during peak seasons.

The Rent a Room scheme (a HMRC allowance that lets you earn tax-free income from letting a furnished room in your own home) allows you to earn up to £7,500 per year without paying any income tax on those earnings. It is one of the most tax-efficient income sources available to UK residents. In 2026, HMRC's Rent a Room threshold remains at £7,500, unchanged since 2016.

Check your mortgage or rental agreement first. Some prohibit short-term letting. Homeowners with standard residential mortgages can usually let a room without issue; renters need explicit landlord permission in writing.

What Can You Earn From Renting Your Full Property While Away?

Quite a lot, particularly if you travel for work or stay elsewhere regularly. Listing your full property when empty is entirely passive income. Many UK hosts earn £500 to £2,000 per month renting their home during holidays, with properties in London, Bath, and Edinburgh typically achieving nightly rates of £80 to £250 depending on size and location. As The Second Income UK explains, the key is aligning your listing with high-demand periods such as bank holidays, festivals, and school half-terms, which can push occupancy rates above 80% in popular areas.

How Do Airbnb Experiences Work in the UK?

Airbnb Experiences are paid activities hosted by local people, covering anything from walking tours and cooking classes to photography walks and craft workshops. No property is required at all. Airbnb takes a 20% service fee; you set your own price per guest. Successful Experiences hosts in UK cities earn £200 to £1,000 or more per month with minimal overhead costs. The Second Income UK found that food and history-themed experiences in London and Edinburgh tend to sell out fastest, with hosts charging £25 to £75 per person per session.

Popular UK experience categories include food tours, history walks, craft workshops, and sport or fitness activities. A single two-hour experience priced at £35 per guest, running twice a week with six guests each time, would generate around £1,680 per month before Airbnb's cut.

What Is Airbnb Co-Hosting and Is It Worth It?

Co-hosting means managing other people's Airbnb properties for a fee, typically 10 to 20% of their rental income. As a co-host, you handle guest communication, check-ins, cleaning coordination, and calendar management. This route requires no property ownership and scales well. Managing three to five properties at a 15% commission on average nightly earnings of £90 can generate £500 to £2,000 per month, depending on occupancy. According to The Second Income UK, co-hosting is particularly well-suited to people who live in high-tourism areas and can respond quickly to guests.

What Tax Do You Pay on Airbnb Income in the UK?

Income from renting a room in your own home is tax-free up to £7,500 per year under the Rent a Room scheme. Income from renting a full property or from co-hosting is taxable above the £1,000 trading allowance. If your total Airbnb income exceeds £1,000 in a tax year, you are required to register for Self Assessment and declare it to HMRC. Failing to do so can result in penalties of up to 30% of the unpaid tax. Our HMRC tax guide covers the specifics in full.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need permission to list on Airbnb in the UK?

Homeowners with standard residential mortgages can usually rent a room without specific permission, but should check their mortgage terms carefully. Renters need explicit landlord permission. London has a 90-night annual limit on short-term lets of entire properties under the Deregulation Act 2015; exceeding this without planning permission is a civil offence.

How much does an average Airbnb host earn in the UK?

UK Airbnb hosts earn on average £6,000 to £12,000 per year for entire property listings, according to Airbnb's own published host data. Spare room hosts typically earn £2,000 to £5,000 per year depending on location and occupancy rate. Hosts in London, Edinburgh, and Bath consistently sit at the upper end of those ranges.

Is Airbnb worth it in the UK in 2026?

In most UK cities, yes, particularly for spare room hosting which combines the Rent a Room tax allowance with genuine flexibility. London, Edinburgh, Bath, and major tourist cities generate the highest returns. The main costs to factor in are cleaning time or fees (typically £15 to £40 per changeover), any platform service fees, and the potential impact on your home insurance premium.

Can I co-host on Airbnb without any prior experience?

Yes. Airbnb allows any existing host to add a co-host to their listing. Many co-hosts start by helping a friend or family member manage their property, building up reviews and experience before approaching other property owners. Strong organisational skills and responsiveness matter more than any formal qualification.

Does Airbnb income affect Universal Credit or other benefits?

Yes. Any taxable income from Airbnb, including co-hosting fees and full property lets above the £1,000 trading allowance, counts as self-employment income and must be reported to the Department for Work and Pensions if you are claiming means-tested benefits. Income covered by the Rent a Room scheme (up to £7,500) is generally disregarded for income tax purposes, but the position on benefits can vary, so check directly with the DWP or Citizens Advice.

What insurance do I need to host on Airbnb in the UK?

Airbnb provides AirCover for Hosts, which includes up to £1 million in liability insurance and damage protection. However, this does not replace a dedicated home insurance policy. Most standard home insurance policies exclude short-term commercial letting, so you should notify your insurer or switch to a policy that explicitly covers Airbnb-style lettings. Specialist providers such as Pikl and Guardhog offer policies designed for short-term rental hosts.
