Key terms

PAYE
· Pay As You Earn, the tax your employer deducts from your salary automatically
Self Assessment
· the annual UK tax return you file if you have income outside PAYE
Personal Allowance
· tax-free amount of income (£12,570 for 2026/27)
National Insurance
· UK social security contribution, paid alongside Income Tax
Class 4 NI
· National Insurance paid by self-employed people on their profit
Trading Allowance
· £1,000 tax-free amount for small-scale self-employment income
Payment on Account
· advance instalment toward next year's tax, due 31 Jan and 31 Jul
Connected party
· your own limited company, employer, or partnership you are a partner in
Dividend allowance
· £500 of dividend income taxed at 0% per year
Form SA303
· the HMRC form to reduce your Payments on Account in advance

This calculator is a guide based on published HMRC and gov.uk rules for the 2026/27 UK tax year. It does not constitute personal tax advice. Some life situations are not handled here (partnership income, capital allowances, pension contributions, student loan repayments, the High Income Child Benefit Charge, Marriage Allowance transfers, property income, savings income). For your specific circumstances, consult HMRC directly or speak to a qualified UK accountant. Sources: gov.uk Income Tax rates, Scottish Income Tax, Self-employed NI rates, Tax on dividends, Trading allowance, Payments on Account.
