First, decide if you need to register

1

"Gross" means everything that landed in your account before any expenses are taken off. If under £1,000, you do not need to register (covered by the £1,000 trading allowance). Check the Trading Allowance Calculator if you are unsure.

2

Other triggers force registration too: rental income over £1,000, dividend income over £500, large capital gains, partner of a partnership, High Income Child Benefit Charge. See gov.uk: who must send a tax return.

Gather your information

3

Format: AB 12 34 56 C. Find on payslip, P60, or HMRC letter. No NI number? Apply at gov.uk/apply-national-insurance-number first. Write yours into the panel above.

4

10-digit number, e.g. 1234567890. You only have one if you have filed an SA return before. Forgotten yours? Find it at gov.uk/find-utr-number. New to SA? You will get one once you register (step 7). Write yours into the panel above when it arrives.

5

Not the day you first earned money. The day you started offering your service or product. Backdating much further than a year may raise questions. Write yours into the panel above.

6

If you have moved within 3 years, previous addresses too. HMRC uses these for identity matching. Write yours into the panel above.

Register online

7

Pick the "self-employed" path. Free, 10-15 minutes. You will be asked for steps 3-6. Deadline: 5 October 2027 for 2026/27 income.

8

If you do not have one, you will be prompted. Keep user ID and password somewhere secure, you need them every year. Write your Gateway user ID into the panel above.

9

HMRC posts your UTR, then sends an activation code separately (1-2 more weeks). Activate within 28 days of receiving the code or it expires.

Set up for next year

10

2026/27 online return due by 31 January 2028. Tax due same date. Set a reminder for 1 December 2027 to start preparing.

11

HMRC requires records for at least 5 years after the 31 January filing deadline (so until 31 January 2033 for 2026/27 records). Paper, photos, or accounting software all fine.

12

Not legally required for sole traders but makes record-keeping dramatically easier. Mixing personal and business funds is the single biggest cause of disorganised SA returns.
