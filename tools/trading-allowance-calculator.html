What is the trading allowance?

The trading allowance is a tax exemption set by HMRC. For the 2026/27 tax year it is £1,000 of gross trading income per person, per tax year. It applies to most small-scale self-employment and casual income: online selling, freelance work, content creation, ride-share driving, food delivery, hobbies that earn money, and most other side hustle activities.

If your gross trading income is at or below £1,000, you do not need to register for Self Assessment in respect of that income, and you do not owe Income Tax or Class 4 National Insurance on it. Above £1,000, you must register and choose between claiming the £1,000 allowance OR your actual expenses, never both.

· your own limited company, your employer, or a partnership you are a partner in
